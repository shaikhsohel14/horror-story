üèöÔ∏è The Abandoned Asylum

The rusty gates of Blackwood Asylum creaked open as five friends stepped inside. "This place has been abandoned for 40 years," Jake whispered, his flashlight cutting through the darkness.

Sarah felt a chill run down her spine. "Did you hear that?" A faint scratching echoed from somewhere deep within the building. "Probably just rats," Mike assured her, but his voice trembled.

As they ventured deeper, patient files lay scattered across the floor. Emily picked one up. "Listen to this: 'Patient shows signs of extreme aggression. Claims to hear voices telling him to wait. Wait for what?'"

The scratching grew louder. It was coming from the basement. Against their better judgment, they descended the stairs. The door at the bottom was covered in scratches from the inside.

"We should leave," Sarah pleaded. But it was too late. The door behind them slammed shut. In the darkness, they heard it‚Äîdozens of footsteps shuffling toward them.

"They've been waiting," a voice rasped from the shadows. "Waiting for new patients to join them..."

The asylum was never abandoned. It was quarantined.

ü™û The Mirror's Reflection

Laura couldn't resist the antique mirror at the estate sale. Its ornate frame seemed to whisper promises of beauty and truth. "Careful with that one," the elderly seller warned. "It shows things that should stay hidden."

That night, Laura hung it in her bedroom. As she brushed her hair, she noticed something odd. Her reflection moved a second too slow, as if lagging behind reality.

Days passed, and the mirror's strangeness grew. Laura saw shadows in the reflection that weren't in her room. Dark figures standing just behind her, their faces obscured.

One night, she woke to find herself standing before the mirror. But her reflection wasn't looking back‚Äîit was looking at something behind her. Laura slowly turned around.

Nothing was there. When she looked back at the mirror, her reflection was smiling. A smile she hadn't made. The reflection raised a finger to its lips. "Shhh... they can only see you through me."

Laura tried to run, but her legs wouldn't move. In the mirror, countless pale hands reached out from the darkness behind her reflection, pulling it back into the glass.

The next morning, Laura's roommate found her standing motionless before the mirror, her eyes vacant. In the reflection, something else stared back.

üå≤ Whispers in the Woods

The hiking trail was supposed to be beginner-friendly. But Marcus and his friends took a wrong turn, and now the forest had swallowed them whole.

"Marcus..." The whisper came from everywhere and nowhere. "Did you guys hear that?" he asked, but his friends shook their heads, continuing to argue about which direction to take.

"Marcus... come here..." The voice was clearer now, almost melodic. It seemed to come from deeper in the woods. Against his better judgment, Marcus followed it, leaving his friends behind.

The trees grew denser, their branches forming a canopy that blocked out the sun. "Marcus... closer..." He pushed through the undergrowth until he reached a clearing.

In the center stood a circle of ancient stones. On each stone, a name was carved. His blood ran cold when he saw the last one: Marcus Chen - Today's Date.

"You found us," the voices chorused, no longer whispers but a cacophony of screams. The ground beneath the stones began to crack open.

His friends never found him. But sometimes, hikers report hearing a new voice in the woods, calling their names...
